INDIA DATA PROTECTION & IT ACT COMPLIANCE REFERENCE
Applicable to: AI-driven systems processing personal/sensitive personal data of Indian residents.

=======================================================================
PART A: INFORMATION TECHNOLOGY ACT, 2000 (IT Act 2000) — Amended 2008
=======================================================================

SECTION 43A — Compensation for failure to protect data
Rule: A body corporate that possesses, deals, or handles any sensitive personal data
or information, and is negligent in implementing reasonable security practices,
causing wrongful loss or wrongful gain to any person, shall be liable to pay
damages by way of compensation.
Applicable when: Any AI system that stores, processes, or transmits applicant
financial/personal data without documented security practices.
Penalty: Unlimited civil liability for damages caused.

SECTION 66C — Punishment for identity theft
Rule: Whoever, fraudulently or dishonestly makes use of the electronic signature,
password, or any other unique identification feature of any other person shall be
punished with imprisonment up to 3 years and a fine up to Rs. 1 lakh.
Applicable when: AI systems that accept applicant identity credentials without
adequate verification (e.g., Aadhaar, PAN, digital signatures) are at risk of
enabling identity theft.

SECTION 66E — Violation of privacy
Rule: Whoever, intentionally or knowingly captures, publishes, or transmits the
image of a private area of any person without his or her consent shall be punished
with imprisonment up to 3 years or a fine up to Rs. 2 lakh.
Applicable when: Any biometric or facial data collected during AI-based KYC.

SECTION 72 — Breach of confidentiality and privacy
Rule: Any person who has secured access to any electronic record, book, register,
correspondence, information, document, or other material and discloses such material
to any other person without the consent of the person concerned shall be punished
with imprisonment up to 2 years or a fine up to Rs. 1 lakh.
Applicable when: AI systems that share applicant data with third parties
(credit bureaus, marketing firms) without explicit consent.

SECTION 72A — Punishment for disclosure of information in breach of lawful contract
Rule: Any person including an intermediary who, while providing services under the
terms of a lawful contract, has secured access to any material containing personal
information about another person, discloses such material without the consent of the
person concerned, is liable for imprisonment up to 3 years or a fine up to Rs. 5 lakh.
Applicable when: Loan processors, analytics vendors, or partners accessing
applicant data must have explicit consent agreements.

RULE 2(1)(i) — Definition of Sensitive Personal Data or Information (SPDI)
IT (Reasonable Security Practices and Procedures and Sensitive Personal Data or
Information) Rules, 2011 — Rule 2(1)(i)

"Sensitive personal data or information" of a person means such personal information
which consists of information relating to:
  1. Passwords
  2. Financial information such as bank account or credit card or debit card or
     other payment instrument details
  3. Physical, physiological and mental health condition
  4. Sexual orientation
  5. Medical records and history
  6. Biometric information
  7. Any detail relating to the above clauses as provided to body corporate for
     providing service
  8. Any of the information received under above clauses by body corporate for
     processing, stored or processed under lawful contract or otherwise

PROVIDED that any information that is freely available or accessible in public
domain or furnished under the Right to Information Act, 2005 or any other law for
the time being in force shall NOT be regarded as sensitive personal data or
information for the purposes of these rules.

IMPORTANT CLASSIFICATION NOTE:
The following are NOT SPDI under Rule 2(1)(i):
  - Name, age, gender, demographic group, religion, nationality
  - Email address, phone number, residential address
  - Loan amount requested (applicant-provided figure, not a financial account detail)
  - Income stated by applicant (applicant-provided figure, not a financial account detail)
  - Credit score (derived metric, not a financial account detail)
  - Employment status, loan purpose, description
  - Application ID, loan status, boolean flags

The following ARE SPDI under Rule 2(1)(i):
  - Bank account number (financial account detail enabling account access)
  - Credit card number (financial account detail enabling account access)
  - Debit card number (financial account detail enabling account access)
  - Health condition, medical history, mental health status (health condition)
  - Biometric data, fingerprint, iris scan, face scan (biometric information)
  - Password, PIN, secret question answer (passwords)
  - Sexual orientation

RULE 2(1)(w) — Definition of Personal Data
"Personal data" means any data about or relating to a natural person who is
directly or indirectly identifiable, having regard to any characteristic, trait,
attribute or any other feature of the identity of such natural person, or any
combination of such features, or any combination of such features with any other
information.

RULE 4 — Body Corporate to provide policy for privacy and disclosure of information
Every body corporate or person on its behalf who collects, receives, possess, stores,
deals or handles information of provider of information, shall provide a privacy
policy for handling of or dealing in personal information including sensitive personal
data or information and ensure that the same is available for view by such providers
of information who have provided such information under lawful contract.

RULE 5 & 6 — IT (Reasonable Security Practices and Procedures and Sensitive Personal
Data or Information) Rules, 2011
Sensitive Personal Data or Information (SPDI) includes:
  - Passwords
  - Financial information (bank account, credit card, debit card details)
  - Physical, physiological, or mental health condition
  - Sexual orientation
  - Medical records and history
  - Biometric information
Rule 5.1: Collect SPDI only for a lawful purpose connected with a function or activity.
Rule 5.2: Must maintain a privacy policy for handling or dealing with personal information.
Rule 5.3: Must take consent in writing or by fax or email before collecting sensitive
personal data or information.
Rule 5.4: The information collected must be used only for the purpose for which it was collected.
Rule 5.5: Must not retain SPDI for longer than is required for the purposes for which it was collected.
Rule 5.6: Must allow the data subject to review, correct, or amend information as necessary.
Rule 5.7: Must allow data subjects to review the information they have provided and withdraw
consent. Upon withdrawal, the body corporate shall not use the data further.
Rule 6.1: Disclose SPDI to third parties only with prior permission from the data subject.
Rule 6.2: Third parties receiving SPDI must maintain the same level of data protection
as the original body corporate.

PART B: DIGITAL PERSONAL DATA PROTECTION ACT, 2023 (DPDPA 2023)
=======================================================================

SECTION 2 — Definitions
- Data Principal: The individual to whom personal data relates.
- Data Fiduciary: Any person who determines the purpose and means of processing personal data.
- Personal Data: Any data about an individual who is identifiable by or in relation to such data.
- Processing: Collection, storage, use, sharing, disclosure, deletion of personal data.
- Consent Manager: A registered entity that enables Data Principals to give, manage,
  review, and withdraw consent.

SECTION 4 — Grounds for processing personal data
Rule: Personal data may be processed only for a lawful purpose for which the Data
Principal has given consent, or for a legitimate use.
Applicable when: Every piece of applicant data processed by the AI must have a
documented lawful basis.
Violation: Processing without consent or legitimate use is unlawful under this section.

SECTION 5 — Notice
Rule: Before seeking consent, a Data Fiduciary must give a notice containing:
  (a) The personal data to be collected and the purpose of processing
  (b) The manner in which the Data Principal may exercise rights
  (c) The manner in which a complaint may be made to the Data Protection Board
Applicable when: Loan application intake form must clearly disclose what data is collected.
Violation: Collecting data without prior notice is a Section 5 violation.

SECTION 6 — Consent
Rule 6.1: Consent must be free, specific, informed, unconditional, and unambiguous
with a clear affirmative action.
Rule 6.2: Consent must be limited to personal data necessary for the purpose.
Rule 6.3: Where consent is given to a Consent Manager, the Data Fiduciary shall be
accountable for demonstrating that consent was given.
Rule 6.4: Consent may be withdrawn at any time. Processing up to withdrawal remains lawful.
Rule 6.5: The Data Principal must be provided a mechanism to withdraw consent as easily
as it was given.
Rule 6.6: If Data Principal is a child, consent of parent or guardian is required.
Applicable when: Checkbox consent for SPDI processing is mandatory. If spdi_consent
is false and SPDI fields are present, this is a Section 6 violation.

SECTION 7 — Legitimate uses (processing without consent)
Covers: Compliance with court judgments, state functions, medical emergencies,
employment purposes, and public interest activities.
Note: Loan processing by a registered NBFC or bank may qualify under legitimate use
if the processing is strictly necessary for the loan contract.

SECTION 8 — General obligations of Data Fiduciary
Rule 8.1: Ensure accuracy and completeness of personal data.
Rule 8.2: Implement appropriate security safeguards to prevent personal data breach.
Rule 8.3: Implement appropriate technical and organisational measures.
Rule 8.4: Notify the Data Protection Board and affected Data Principals in the event
of a personal data breach, in such form and manner as may be prescribed.
Rule 8.5: Not retain personal data beyond the period necessary for the stated purpose.
Rule 8.6: Publish the business contact information of a Data Protection Officer or
other person able to respond to Data Principals.
Rule 8.7: Erase personal data upon the Data Principal's request or when the
purpose is no longer served.
Rule 8.9: Display data retention policy prominently.
Applicable when: AI system must have breach notification, erasure, and retention
procedures documented.

SECTION 9 — Processing of personal data of children
Rule: Data Fiduciary must obtain verifiable consent from a parent or guardian
before processing data of a child (under 18).
Rule: Data Fiduciary must not track, monitor, or engage in behavioural targeting
of children or advertise to children based on their personal data.
Rule: Processing that could harm a child's well-being is prohibited.
Applicable when: Any applicant with age < 18 triggers this section. Parental consent
must be documented. children_data_risk = true if age < 18.

SECTION 10 — Significant Data Fiduciary obligations
Rule: The Central Government may notify any Data Fiduciary as a Significant Data
Fiduciary based on volume of data processed, sensitivity of data, national security
risk, or risk to rights of Data Principals.
Obligations include:
  - Appoint a Data Protection Officer based in India
  - Appoint an independent Data Auditor
  - Conduct periodic Data Protection Impact Assessments (DPIA)
Applicable when: High-volume AI systems processing large amounts of SPDI must
conduct DPIA. dpia_required = true if spdi_count >= 5.

SECTION 11 — Consent of the Data Principal
Every request for consent shall be presented separately from any other matter,
in a clear and plain language. The request must specify the personal data sought
and the purpose of processing.

SECTION 12 — Right of the Data Principal to access information
Rule: Data Principal has the right to obtain:
  (a) A summary of personal data being processed and the processing activities
  (b) Identities of all Data Fiduciaries with whom data has been shared
  (c) Any other information related to their personal data as may be prescribed.
Applicable when: System must provide a /rights/access endpoint responding within
30 days.

SECTION 13 — Right to correction and erasure
Rule: Data Principal may request:
  (a) Correction of inaccurate or misleading personal data
  (b) Completion of incomplete personal data
  (c) Updating of personal data
  (d) Erasure of personal data that is no longer necessary
Applicable when: System must provide a /rights/erasure endpoint. Erasure requests
must be honoured unless retention is required by law.

SECTION 14 — Right of grievance redressal
Rule: Data Principal has the right to have grievances redressed within a reasonable
time not exceeding 30 days. Data Fiduciary must publish grievance redressal mechanism.

SECTION 16 — Transfer of personal data outside India
Rule: Personal data may be transferred outside India only to countries notified
by the Central Government as permitted recipients.
Rule: Cross-border transfer of SPDI requires additional safeguards.
Applicable when: If data_processing_location, analytics_vendor, or cloud_storage
field contains a reference to a non-India location such as USA, UK, Europe, AWS,
GCP, Azure, or any foreign country, this section is triggered.
cross_border_risk = true if any location field contains a non-India keyword.
Violation: Transferring data to a non-approved country is a Section 16 violation.

SECTION 17 — Exemptions
The Central Government may exempt certain Data Fiduciaries from provisions
of this Act in the interest of sovereignty, security, or public order.

SECTIONS 25 to 40 — Data Protection Board of India (DPBI)
The Board is established to adjudicate on personal data breaches and complaints.
Penalties:
  - Failure to implement security safeguards (Section 8): up to Rs. 250 crore
  - Failure to notify a personal data breach: up to Rs. 200 crore
  - Non-compliance with children's data rules (Section 9): up to Rs. 200 crore
  - Non-compliance with significant data fiduciary obligations (Section 10): up to Rs. 150 crore
  - General non-compliance with other provisions: up to Rs. 50 crore

PART C: DIGITAL SIGNATURE & ELECTRONIC GOVERNANCE (IT Act 2000, Ch. II & VI)
=======================================================================

SECTION 3 — Authentication of electronic records
Electronic records may be authenticated by use of an asymmetric crypto system
and hash function (Digital Signature). Any AI system accepting digitally signed
documents must verify the digital signature via a licensed Certifying Authority (CA).

SECTION 5 — Legal recognition of digital signatures
Digital signatures on electronic records are legally equivalent to handwritten
signatures. Loan contracts signed digitally are legally binding.

SECTION 6 — Use of digital signatures by Government
Government-facing AI systems (e.g., Aadhaar-linked loan verifications) must
comply with NIC/CCA standards for electronic governance.

SECTION 35 — Certifying Authority
Only a licensed Certifying Authority (CA) can issue Digital Signature Certificates.
AI systems must not accept self-signed certificates for financial transactions.

PART D: AMENDMENT TO INDIAN EVIDENCE ACT 1872 (via IT Act 2000)
=======================================================================

SECTION 65B — Admissibility of electronic records
Electronic records are admissible as evidence if accompanied by a certificate
from a responsible official stating:
  (a) The computer output was produced during a period of regular use
  (b) The computer was operating properly during that period
  (c) The information in the electronic record reproduces information in the computer
Applicable when: AI-generated loan decisions stored as electronic records must
meet Section 65B requirements to be admissible as evidence in disputes.
